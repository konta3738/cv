Profile

M.Sc. student in Language Science and Technology at Saarland University and Research Assistant at DFG SFB 1102, working on mechanistic and evaluation-driven understanding of large language models, with a focus on mechanistic interpretability, architectural biases, controllability, and language- and modality-related internal representations. My research combines neuron-level analysis, logit lens methods, causal interventions, and representation alignment to study how language biases emerge across layers, languages, and modalities. I am particularly interested in connecting mechanistic findings to user-relevant evaluation signals such as hallucination and response reliability.

Research direction: Understanding how hallucinations arise from internal representations in LLMs, and how mechanistic insights can inform controllable and user-aligned generation.
